## Overview
A modeling and analysis project that predicts churn risk and customer value (LTV) and converts those signals into operational prioritization.

## Problem
Retention teams cannot treat all customers equally:
- Churn probability alone can mis-rank priorities if the customer value is ignored
- High-risk but low-value users can consume resources with limited ROI
- Predictions are not useful unless they translate into concrete targeting and actions

## Diagnosis
The core bottleneck was not just predictive performance, but decision readiness:
- Churn risk and value signals must be combined into a single prioritization frame
- Without interpretability, teams struggle to trust and act on model outputs
- Different cohorts exhibit different churn drivers, making one-size-fits-all actions inefficient

## Approach
### Churn × LTV prioritization framework
- Defined actionable segments using churn probability and expected value
- Prioritized “High risk × High value” customers for retention intervention

### Segment-level interpretation
- Compared cohorts/segments to understand key drivers and differences
- Delivered explanations aligned with operational decision-making

### Action-oriented outputs
- Produced ranked target lists and simple rules for execution
- Mapped segments to candidate interventions (retention offers, outreach, support)

## Result
- Built a prioritization system that balances churn risk with expected value
- Converted predictions into execution-ready targeting artifacts for operations

## Key insight
The success of churn modeling is measured by decisions and actions, not by prediction metrics alone. A good system connects risk, value, and explainability into a workflow teams can execute.
